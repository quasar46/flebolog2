Varicose veins of the lower extremities

Valve pathology and the appearance of reverse blood flow through the saphenous veins of the legs

ANATOMY

The venous system of the lower extremities consists of deep and saphenous veins

90%

blood moves through deep veins

10%

blood moves through the saphenous (superficial) veins

physiology

Blood through the veins must move towards the heart

Healthy vein

Veins contain valves that direct blood flow in only one direction (toward the heart).

When standing, the blood in the veins of the legs always moves only upward, against the force of gravity.

Vein affected by varicose veins

Movement of blood in both directions.

REFLUX

reverse movement of blood when valves are malfunctioning.

reasons

Genetic predisposition

breakdowns in various genes

Previous venous thrombosis of the saphenous veins
Pathology of deep veins
Plastic surgery

Treatment
methods

The main goal of our treatment is to stop the reverse movement of blood (reflux) through the veins

At the moment, there are no methods for restoring the valve apparatus of the saphenous veins.

The only effective way to treat varicose veins of the lower extremities is to remove the dysfunctional vein.

Sclerotherapy

Non-surgical method for treating varicose veins. A biological substance is injected into the vein, which glues the walls of the vessel and stops improper blood flow
